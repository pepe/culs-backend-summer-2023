Explain quantum computing in simple terms

Quantum computing is a type of computing that uses quantum bits, or qubits, to perform operations on data. Unlike classical computers, which use bits that can either be a 0 or a 1, qubits can represent a 0, a 1, or both at the same time. This allows quantum computers to perform certain calculations much faster than classical computers.
